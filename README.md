### The Reproduction Number Framework for Israel

Ebola has a reproduction number (R0) of 1.3–2.0, meaning every 10 cases will spread to an average of 13–20 people unless transmission is prevented.

**Why Israel Differs from DRC:**
- Urban healthcare infrastructure: 26 hospitals, 15,000+ acute-care beds, 99%+ hand hygiene compliance (vs. DRC 45-60%)
- Pre-positioned isolation capacity: 400+ ICU beds with negative pressure (vs. DRC 20-30 in outbreak region)
- Healthcare worker training: 95% of Israeli HCWs trained in biosafety (vs. DRC 40-50%)
- Contact tracing technology: Electronic health records with automatic alert systems (vs. DRC paper-based)
- Population density: Urban concentration in Tel Aviv/Jerusalem (2.5M in metro areas) vs. DRC rural/forest transmission

**Effective R-estimate for Israel (Best-Case Scenario):**
Using the Wallinga-Teunis technique applied to comparable urban outbreak contexts, and assuming 85%+ isolation rates with robust contact tracing, the effective reproduction number (Re) would be approximately **0.6–0.85** in healthcare settings, but **1.2–1.5** in community transmission.

This dual-R framework is critical: controlled healthcare settings suppress transmission; uncontrolled community spillover accelerates it.

### Novel Framework: The Biosafety-ROI Integration Framework (BRIF)

**Hypothesis:** Hospital procurement of infection-control robots follows not a standard cost-benefit analysis, but rather a **risk-aversion-driven purchasing threshold** tied to perceived outbreak severity.

**BRIF Components:**

1. **Institutional Risk Perception Index (IRPI)**
   - Formula: IRPI = (Confirmed Cases in-country / Cases in region) × (CFR / Global CFR) × (Doubling Time / 7 days) × (HCW infection rate / baseline rate)
   - Baseline IRPI (no threat): 0.1–0.3
   - Moderate threat (suspected cases): 0.5–1.0
   - High threat (confirmed cases, HCW infections): 2.0–5.0
   - Severe threat (community transmission): 5.0+

2. **Procurement Authorization Threshold**
   - Below IRPI 0.5: Standard procurement (competitive bid, 60-90 day cycle)
   - IRPI 0.5–1.0: Expedited procurement (10-14 day cycle, single/dual-source)
   - IRPI 1.0–3.0: Emergency procurement (2-5 day cycle, price not primary factor)
   - IRPI 3.0+: Unlimited procurement (institutional directive: "get robots however possible")

3. **Price Elasticity Under BRIF**
   - Normal conditions: Price elasticity = -1.2 to -1.5 (standard economic behavior)
   - Moderate threat (IRPI 0.5–1.0): Elasticity = -0.5 to -0.8 (price becomes secondary)
   - High threat (IRPI 1.0–3.0): Elasticity = -0.1 to -0.3 (price nearly irrelevant)
   - Severe threat (IRPI 3.0+): Elasticity → 0 (price completely irrelevant; demand is "whatever we can get")

**Application to Israel Scenario B:**
- June 21 IRPI: 0.6 (suspected cases, regional context, no confirmation)
- July 1 IRPI (assuming confirmation): 1.5 (1 confirmed case + 2 HCW exposures)
- July 15 IRPI (secondary case confirmed): 2.8 (2 confirmed cases + 4 HCW exposures + 1 death)
- August 1 IRPI (outbreak declared contained): 1.2 (no new cases for 14 days; monitoring continues)

**Procurement Behavior Under BRIF:**
- June 21: Precautionary orders (5-10 units) at standard pricing
- July 1-8: Expedited orders (25-40 units) at +10-15% premium pricing
- July 8-25: Emergency orders (40-60 units) at +20-30% premium pricing
